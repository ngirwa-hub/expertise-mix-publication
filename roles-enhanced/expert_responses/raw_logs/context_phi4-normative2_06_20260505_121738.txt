1. **Interoperability Challenges**  
   The integration of DC systems with existing AC infrastructure often faces compatibility issues, leading to technical complexities and increased costs for upgrades and retrofits.

2. **Economic Uncertainty**  
   Fluctuating market conditions and the uncertain return on investment can deter stakeholders from committing to large-scale DC projects without clear financial incentives or regulatory support.

3. **Lack of Skilled Workforce**  
   There is a shortage of professionals trained in DC technologies, which hampers project implementation and maintenance due to limited expertise available for installation and troubleshooting.

4. **Regulatory Inertia**  
   Slow adaptation of regulations to accommodate new DC standards can delay deployment efforts, as existing codes are often tailored specifically for AC systems, creating bureaucratic hurdles.

5. **Public Perception and Awareness**  
   Limited public understanding of the benefits of DC over traditional AC systems may lead to resistance or lack of enthusiasm, affecting consumer acceptance and adoption rates in residential and commercial settings.
