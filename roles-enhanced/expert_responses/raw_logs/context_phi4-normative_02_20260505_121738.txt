1. **Grid Compatibility Challenges**
   - Integrating DC systems with existing AC infrastructure requires significant modifications, which can be costly and complex. The differences in frequency standards (50 Hz vs. 60 Hz) further complicate seamless integration.

2. **Standardization Gaps**
   - Lack of universally accepted standards for low and medium voltage DC systems hampers widespread adoption. Inconsistent regulations across regions create uncertainty for manufacturers and developers regarding compliance requirements.

3. **Economic Viability Concerns**
   - Initial capital costs for transitioning to DC technologies can be prohibitive, especially in sectors with tight profit margins. Without clear cost-benefit analyses demonstrating long-term savings, stakeholders may hesitate to invest.

4. **Workforce Skill Deficit**
   - A shortage of skilled professionals familiar with DC systems poses a barrier to adoption. Training programs and educational initiatives are necessary to equip the workforce with the required expertise for installation, maintenance, and troubleshooting.

5. **Public Perception and Acceptance**
   - Limited public awareness and understanding of DC technologies can lead to resistance or indifference. Effective communication strategies are needed to educate consumers about the benefits and safety of DC systems to foster acceptance and demand.
