1. **Scalability Challenges**
    - Lack of standardized components for large-scale DC networks, leading to higher costs and technical challenges in scaling up systems.

2. **Interoperability Issues**
    - Incompatibility between different DC technologies, hardware components, and existing power grids can create significant hurdles in adopting DC infrastructure across sectors.

3. **Regulatory Uncertainty**
    - Inconsistent regulations and policy frameworks for DC systems at the regional or national level may deter investment and hinder widespread adoption.

4. **Public Perception**
    - Negative perceptions around the safety and reliability of DC systems, particularly in areas with a history of power-related incidents, can slow down adoption rates.

5. **Inadequate Skilled Labor**
    - The lack of skilled professionals trained in designing, operating, and maintaining DC systems may limit the growth and implementation of these technologies across various industries.
