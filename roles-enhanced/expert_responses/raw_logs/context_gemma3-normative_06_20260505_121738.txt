## New Barriers to DC Adoption - Expert Elicitation

Here are 5 new barriers to DC adoption, considering my Environmental, Regulatory & Socioeconomic Advisor role:

1.  **Harmonized International Standards Gap:** Lack of globally recognized and legally binding standards for DC low and medium voltage systems creates uncertainty for manufacturers, installers, and project developers, hindering cross-border deployments.
2.  **Contractor Skillset Deficit:** Existing electrical contractors lack the specialized training and experience required for DC system design, installation, and maintenance, leading to increased risk and potentially compromised performance.
3.  **Community Perception & Aesthetic Concerns:** DC infrastructure (cabling, converters) may face public resistance due to perceived visual impact, noise, or concerns about electromagnetic fields, requiring proactive community engagement and mitigation strategies.
4.  **Legacy Infrastructure Retrofit Costs:** Integrating DC into existing facilities with significant AC infrastructure presents substantial retrofit costs and logistical challenges, potentially outweighing the benefits in some cases.
5.  **Lifecycle Environmental Impact Assessment:** A full lifecycle assessment of DC systems, including converter manufacturing and disposal, is needed to ensure that the shift to DC genuinely delivers environmental benefits compared to AC alternatives.
