## Unforeseen DC Adoption Barriers

Here are five barriers to DC adoption, considering my role as a Low Voltage Systems & Automation Engineer (SME) focused on project delivery and technical support:

1. **Lack of Standardized Component Interoperability:** While standards are being developed, a true lack of interoperability between different DC component manufacturers will hinder system integration and create vendor lock-in.
2. **Limited Availability of Trained Personnel:** The current workforce lacks sufficient expertise in DC system design, installation, and maintenance, creating a skills gap that will impede project execution.
3. **Retrofit Costs & Disruption:** Integrating DC into existing infrastructure (especially in industry and buildings) will be significantly more expensive and disruptive than new builds, limiting adoption in older facilities.
4. **Cybersecurity Risks in DC Microgrids:** The decentralized nature of DC microgrids increases the attack surface for cyber threats, requiring robust security measures and expertise that are not universally available.
5. **Perceived Reliability Concerns:** A lack of long-term operational data and field experience may lead to a perception of lower reliability compared to established AC systems, creating hesitancy among risk-averse stakeholders.
