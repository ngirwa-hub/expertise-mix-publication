1. **Interoperability Challenges**
   - Integration of DC systems with existing AC infrastructure often faces technical hurdles due to differences in standards, which can hinder seamless interoperability and limit the adoption of hybrid systems.

2. **Limited Skilled Workforce**
   - There is a scarcity of professionals trained specifically in DC technologies, leading to challenges in installation, maintenance, and troubleshooting, thereby slowing down deployment rates.

3. **Economic Uncertainty for Small-Scale Applications**
   - For small-scale applications like residential or small commercial buildings, the economic benefits of switching to DC can be uncertain due to high initial costs and lack of financial incentives, deterring potential adopters.

4. **Regulatory Gaps and Inconsistencies**
   - Varying regulations across regions regarding DC systems create a fragmented market landscape, making it difficult for manufacturers and developers to design universally applicable solutions.

5. **Public Perception and Awareness Deficits**
   - Limited awareness and understanding of the benefits and safety of DC among the general public can lead to resistance or hesitancy in adopting DC technologies, particularly in consumer-facing applications like electric vehicles and home installations.
