1. **Grid Integration Complexity**
   - Integrating DC systems with existing AC grids poses technical challenges, requiring advanced grid management solutions and potentially leading to increased costs and complexities during retrofitting.

2. **Standardization Gaps**
   - Lack of universally accepted standards for DC components and systems can hinder interoperability between different manufacturers and applications, complicating adoption across industries.

3. **Economic Uncertainty**
   - Initial investment in DC infrastructure may be high compared to established AC solutions, creating economic uncertainty for stakeholders considering transitioning without clear long-term cost-benefit analyses.

4. **Skill Shortage**
   - A limited workforce with expertise in designing, installing, and maintaining DC systems can delay adoption, as training programs need to catch up with technological advancements.

5. **Regulatory Ambiguity**
   - Unclear or inconsistent regulations regarding DC implementation may deter companies from investing in new technologies due to potential legal and compliance risks.
