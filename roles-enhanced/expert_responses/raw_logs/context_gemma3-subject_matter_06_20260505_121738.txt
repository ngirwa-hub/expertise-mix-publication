## DC Adoption Barriers - Expert Insights

Here are 5 new barriers to DC adoption, considering my role as a Low Voltage Systems & Automation Engineer, SME, focused on project delivery and technical support:

1. **Lack of Standardized Component Interoperability:** Current DC component offerings are fragmented, leading to integration challenges and a lack of guaranteed interoperability between different vendors' equipment. This complicates system design and increases risk.
2. **Limited Availability of Qualified Installers/Commissioning Personnel:** The relative novelty of DC systems means a shortage of technicians and engineers with the specific skillset required for installation, commissioning, and ongoing maintenance.
3. **Cybersecurity Integration Complexity:** Integrating DC systems into existing OT/IT infrastructure introduces new attack vectors and requires specialized cybersecurity expertise to protect data integrity and system reliability.
4. **Retrofit Costs & Disruption:** Replacing existing AC infrastructure with DC systems in existing facilities will be significantly more disruptive and costly than greenfield deployments, hindering adoption in brownfield environments.
5. **Long-Term Reliability Data Gaps:** Limited operational history for DC components and systems means a lack of long-term reliability data, increasing perceived risk for potential adopters and potentially impacting warranty provisions.
