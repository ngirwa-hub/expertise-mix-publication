### New Barriers to DC Adoption

1. **Interoperability Challenges**
   - As DC technologies integrate into existing AC systems, ensuring seamless interoperability can be complex. Differences in standards and protocols across regions and industries may hinder the adoption of DC solutions.

2. **Supply Chain Constraints**
   - The current supply chain is predominantly optimized for AC components. Transitioning to a DC-focused supply chain requires retooling manufacturers and suppliers, which can delay adoption and increase initial costs.

3. **Economic Uncertainty**
   - Fluctuating energy prices and uncertain economic conditions may deter investment in new technologies like DC systems. Companies might be hesitant to commit resources without clear long-term financial benefits.

4. **Lack of Skilled Workforce**
   - There is a shortage of professionals trained specifically in DC technology installation, maintenance, and troubleshooting. This skills gap can slow down the deployment and efficient operation of DC systems.

5. **Data Security Concerns**
   - As DC systems increasingly rely on digital tools and platforms for monitoring and control, concerns about data security and privacy arise. Ensuring robust cybersecurity measures is essential to gain stakeholder trust.
