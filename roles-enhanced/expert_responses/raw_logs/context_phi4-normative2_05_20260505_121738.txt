1. **Interoperability Challenges**
   - Compatibility issues between existing AC infrastructure and new DC systems can hinder seamless integration, leading to increased costs and technical complexities during the transition.

2. **Standardization Gaps**
   - Lack of universal standards for DC technologies across different regions and industries creates confusion and slows down adoption due to varying specifications and safety requirements.

3. **Skilled Workforce Shortage**
   - There is a limited pool of professionals trained in designing, installing, and maintaining DC systems, which can delay projects and increase costs as the workforce needs upskilling or expansion.

4. **Perception and Awareness Barriers**
   - Limited public understanding and acceptance of DC technologies may lead to resistance from consumers and stakeholders, affecting market penetration and investment willingness.

5. **Economic Uncertainty in ROI Calculations**
   - Difficulty in accurately predicting long-term returns on investment due to fluctuating energy prices and evolving technology can deter potential adopters from committing to DC systems.
