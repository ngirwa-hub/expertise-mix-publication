1. **Interoperability Challenges**
   - With diverse standards and protocols across different regions, ensuring interoperability of DC systems can be challenging. This barrier involves integrating new technologies with existing AC infrastructure without compromising performance or safety.

2. **Market Fragmentation**
   - The market for DC components is currently fragmented, which can hinder the development of standardized solutions and economies of scale. This fragmentation complicates sourcing reliable components and discourages investment in DC technology from manufacturers and stakeholders.

3. **Limited Skilled Workforce**
   - There is a shortage of professionals trained specifically in DC technologies, creating a skills gap that hinders implementation and maintenance. Addressing this requires significant investment in training programs to ensure the workforce can effectively deploy and manage DC systems.

4. **Supply Chain Vulnerabilities**
   - Relying on global supply chains for specialized components like high-capacity cables or converters introduces vulnerabilities. Disruptions due to geopolitical issues, trade policies, or logistical challenges can delay projects and increase costs.

5. **Regulatory Complexity**
   - Navigating the complex landscape of regulations across different jurisdictions poses a significant barrier. Harmonizing these regulations is essential to facilitate cross-border implementation and ensure compliance with safety, performance, and environmental standards.
