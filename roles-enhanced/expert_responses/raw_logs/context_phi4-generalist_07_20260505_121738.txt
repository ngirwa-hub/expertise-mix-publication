1. **Economic Viability Concerns**
   - The initial cost of transitioning existing infrastructure from AC to DC can be prohibitive for many organizations, particularly when considering retrofitting older facilities or systems that were not designed with DC compatibility in mind.

2. **Interoperability and Standardization Issues**
   - Lack of standardized components and protocols across different manufacturers and regions hinders seamless integration and interoperability of DC technologies, complicating widespread adoption and system scalability.

3. **Technical Expertise Shortage**
   - There is a limited pool of professionals with the necessary skills and knowledge to design, implement, and maintain DC systems, which can slow down adoption rates and increase project costs due to higher training and hiring expenses.

4. **Public Perception and Awareness**
   - Limited public understanding and acceptance of DC technologies may lead to resistance from stakeholders who are not convinced of its benefits or fearful of potential disruptions during the transition phase.

5. **Regulatory and Policy Uncertainty**
   - Inconsistent or unclear regulations regarding DC systems can create uncertainty for businesses considering adoption, as they may face legal challenges or be unable to secure necessary approvals for implementation.
