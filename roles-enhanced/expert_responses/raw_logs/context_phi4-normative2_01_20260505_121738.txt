Certainly! Here are five potential barriers to DC adoption not previously mentioned:

1. **Interoperability Challenges**
   - Integrating DC systems with existing AC infrastructure can be complex, requiring significant adaptation in terms of compatibility between different voltage systems and equipment.

2. **Economic Viability Concerns**
   - The initial cost for transitioning to DC systems, including the installation of new hardware and potential retrofitting expenses, may deter stakeholders without clear long-term financial benefits.

3. **Regulatory Uncertainty**
   - Inconsistent or evolving regulations across different regions can create uncertainty, making it difficult for organizations to commit to DC technology investments that might not align with future policy changes.

4. **Skill Gaps in Workforce**
   - The current workforce may lack the specialized skills required to design, install, and maintain DC systems effectively, necessitating significant investment in training and education.

5. **Public Perception and Acceptance**
   - There might be a lack of awareness or understanding among the general public about the benefits of DC technology, which could hinder widespread adoption due to resistance or indifference towards new infrastructure changes.
