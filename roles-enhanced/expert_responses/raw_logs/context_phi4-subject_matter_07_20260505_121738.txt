### New Barriers to DC Adoption

1. **Interoperability Challenges**
   - The lack of standardized interfaces between existing AC systems and emerging DC technologies can hinder integration, leading to compatibility issues that complicate widespread adoption.

2. **Limited Supply Chain Readiness**
   - Insufficient manufacturing capacity and supply chain infrastructure for key DC components like cables and converters may lead to cost increases or delays in project timelines, impacting the scalability of DC solutions.

3. **Skill Gaps in Workforce**
   - A shortage of trained professionals skilled in designing, installing, and maintaining DC systems can slow adoption rates as companies struggle to find qualified personnel to support new technology deployments.

4. **Regulatory Uncertainty**
   - Inconsistent or evolving regulations across different regions regarding the use of DC technologies create uncertainty for stakeholders, making it difficult to plan long-term investments confidently.

5. **Perceived Reliability Concerns**
   - Perceptions that AC systems are more reliable due to their historical prevalence can lead to reluctance in adopting DC solutions, despite advancements proving their robustness and efficiency in specific applications.
