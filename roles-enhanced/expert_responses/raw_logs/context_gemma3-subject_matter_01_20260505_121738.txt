## DC Adoption Barriers: Beyond the Obvious

Here are 5 new barriers to DC adoption, considering my Low Voltage Systems & Automation Engineer SME role:

1. **Lack of Standardized Component Lifecycle Management:** DC systems, particularly with varying voltage levels and architectures, will necessitate robust lifecycle management for components. The absence of standardized practices for obsolescence, maintenance, and replacement will increase operational risk and cost.

2. **Cybersecurity Integration Complexity:** Integrating DC systems into existing OT environments significantly expands the attack surface. Current cybersecurity frameworks are largely AC-centric; adapting them to DC architectures requires new approaches and expertise, creating a barrier to secure implementation.

3. **Contractor Skill Gap & Retraining Costs:** Widespread DC adoption requires a significant upskilling of electrical contractors. The current workforce lacks experience with DC systems, leading to increased installation costs and potential quality issues.

4. **Limited Availability of DC-Rated Safety Equipment:** Current safety standards and equipment (e.g., switchgear, enclosures, personal protective equipment) are largely designed for AC. A lack of readily available, certified DC-rated alternatives will hinder safe and compliant implementation.

5. **Interoperability Challenges Across DC Voltage Levels:** As DC systems evolve with varying voltage levels (e.g., 48V, 240V, higher), ensuring seamless interoperability between different components and systems from various vendors will be crucial but technically complex.
