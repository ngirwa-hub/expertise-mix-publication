## New Barriers to DC Adoption - Shift2DC Expert Elicitation

Here are five new barriers to DC adoption, considering my Environmental, Regulatory & Socioeconomic Advisor role and the project’s focus:

1.  **Legacy Infrastructure Compatibility:** Integrating DC systems into existing AC infrastructure presents significant physical and electrical compatibility challenges, requiring complex and costly retrofits or parallel systems.
2.  **Standardized Safety Protocols Gap:** Current safety standards are heavily focused on AC systems. A lack of comprehensive, DC-specific safety guidelines and training can delay adoption and increase liability concerns.
3.  **Community Perception & Aesthetics:** Public perception of DC infrastructure (cabling, converters) could be negative, particularly in densely populated areas, leading to community resistance and permitting delays.
4.  **Contractor Skillset Deficiencies:** A lack of readily available contractors with experience in DC system installation, maintenance, and troubleshooting will hinder project implementation and increase operational risks.
5.  **Long-Term Regulatory Uncertainty:**  While the project aims to establish guidelines, evolving regulations concerning DC grid integration, safety, and interoperability could create long-term investment uncertainty.

1. **Lack of Standardized Component Interoperability**
Current DC component offerings from different manufacturers lack standardized interfaces and communication protocols, hindering seamless integration and system design.

2. **Limited Availability of Skilled Workforce**
A shortage of engineers and technicians with expertise in DC power systems design, installation, and maintenance poses a significant bottleneck for widespread adoption.

3. **Supply Chain Vulnerabilities**
Reliance on specific geographic regions for critical DC components creates vulnerabilities in the supply chain, potentially delaying projects or increasing costs.

4. **Retrofit Challenges & Costs**
Integrating DC systems into existing AC infrastructure presents significant technical and financial hurdles, limiting adoption in older buildings and facilities.

5. **Public Perception & Safety Concerns**
Lack of public understanding about DC technology and potential safety concerns (e.g., perceived higher voltages) can create resistance and delay project approvals.
