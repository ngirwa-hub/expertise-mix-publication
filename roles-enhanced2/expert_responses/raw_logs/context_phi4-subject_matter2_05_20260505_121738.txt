1. **Interoperability Challenges**  
   The integration of DC systems with existing AC infrastructure can present significant interoperability challenges, requiring advanced interface technologies to ensure seamless operation across different voltage levels.

2. **Economic Viability Concerns**  
   Initial costs associated with transitioning to DC infrastructure may be prohibitive for some sectors, particularly when compared to established AC solutions, leading to hesitation in adoption despite long-term benefits.

3. **Lack of Standardization**  
   The absence of universal standards and protocols for DC systems can impede widespread adoption by creating inconsistencies in design, installation, and maintenance practices across regions and industries.

4. **Skilled Workforce Shortage**  
   There is a limited pool of professionals with the technical expertise required to design, install, and maintain DC systems, which could slow down implementation efforts and increase project costs due to training needs.

5. **Public Perception and Acceptance**  
   Despite potential efficiencies, there may be resistance from consumers and stakeholders who are unfamiliar with or skeptical about the benefits of DC technology, impacting its acceptance and integration into everyday applications.
