1. **Interoperability Challenges**
   - New DC solutions may face compatibility issues with existing AC infrastructure, leading to integration challenges across different voltage levels and systems.

2. **Standardization Gaps**
   - The lack of universally accepted standards for DC components and systems can hinder widespread adoption by creating uncertainties in design, safety, and interoperability requirements.

3. **Economic Viability Concerns**
   - Initial costs associated with transitioning to DC technologies may be perceived as prohibitive, especially when compared to established AC solutions without clear short-term financial benefits.

4. **Regulatory Uncertainty**
   - Inconsistent or evolving regulatory frameworks across regions can impede the adoption of DC systems due to uncertainties around compliance, permitting processes, and incentives.

5. **Skill and Knowledge Deficit**
   - A shortage of trained professionals with expertise in DC technologies may slow down implementation and maintenance efforts, limiting the effectiveness and reliability of new deployments.
