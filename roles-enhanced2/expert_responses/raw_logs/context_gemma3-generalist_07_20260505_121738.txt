## New Barriers to DC Adoption

Here are five new barriers to DC adoption, considering my role as a Generalist Energy & Sustainability Analyst focused on data-led reporting, energy analysis, and cross-functional delivery:

1. **Data Harmonization & Interoperability:** Lack of standardized data formats and protocols across DC systems will hinder aggregation, benchmarking, and overall system performance monitoring – critical for reporting and assurance.
2. **Cybersecurity Vulnerabilities:** DC systems, particularly microgrids, introduce new attack vectors. Current cybersecurity frameworks may not adequately address these, requiring significant investment and expertise.
3. **Supply Chain Dependence:** Reliance on specific hardware components and manufacturers could create supply chain vulnerabilities and limit scalability if demand surges.
4. **Skill Gap in Maintenance & Operations:** Existing maintenance and operational teams lack experience with DC systems, leading to potential inefficiencies, higher maintenance costs, and safety concerns.
5. **Retrofit Costs & Disruption:** Integrating DC into existing infrastructure (buildings, ports) will be expensive and disruptive, requiring careful planning and potentially impacting operations.

1. **Lack of Standardized Component Availability**
Limited availability of readily accessible, certified DC components beyond converters hinders scalability and increases project costs.

2. **Retrofit Integration Costs**
Replacing existing AC infrastructure with DC in brownfield sites is significantly more expensive and disruptive than new builds.

3. **Workforce Skill Gap**
Insufficiently trained electrical engineers and technicians familiar with DC systems will impede installation and maintenance.

4. **Cybersecurity Vulnerabilities**
DC microgrids, with their increased connectivity, present novel cybersecurity risks requiring specialized protection strategies.

5. **Interoperability Concerns**
Lack of standardized communication protocols between diverse DC components from different manufacturers limits system integration.
