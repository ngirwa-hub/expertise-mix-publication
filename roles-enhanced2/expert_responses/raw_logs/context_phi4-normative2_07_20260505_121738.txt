1. **Interoperability Challenges**
   - Different manufacturers may produce DC components with varying standards, leading to compatibility issues. This lack of standardization can hinder seamless integration into existing AC systems.

2. **Economic Viability Concerns**
   - The initial costs for transitioning from AC to DC infrastructure might be high. Without clear long-term economic benefits or incentives, organizations may be reluctant to invest in DC technologies.

3. **Workforce Training and Expertise Gaps**
   - There is a shortage of skilled professionals trained specifically in DC systems. This gap can slow down the adoption process as industries struggle to find qualified personnel for installation, maintenance, and troubleshooting.

4. **Regulatory Uncertainty**
   - Existing regulations are primarily designed around AC systems. The uncertainty regarding how new DC technologies will be regulated or integrated into current frameworks could deter potential adopters from making the switch.

5. **Perception of Technological Maturity**
   - Many stakeholders may perceive DC technology as less mature compared to established AC solutions, leading to hesitancy in adopting it for critical applications due to concerns over reliability and performance.
