1. **Interoperability Challenges**
   - The lack of standardized protocols for integrating DC systems with existing AC infrastructure can hinder seamless interoperability, leading to increased costs and complexity during deployment.

2. **Grid Stability Concerns**
   - Transitioning parts of the grid to DC could introduce instability issues, as current infrastructure is predominantly designed for AC. This requires sophisticated control mechanisms to maintain reliability.

3. **Economic Viability Hurdles**
   - The initial investment required for DC technology can be prohibitive, especially when compared to established AC systems. Without clear economic incentives or subsidies, adoption may lag due to cost concerns.

4. **Skill and Knowledge Gaps**
   - There is a shortage of professionals trained in DC technologies, which could slow down implementation efforts. Comprehensive training programs are necessary to build the required workforce expertise.

5. **Public Perception and Acceptance**
   - Limited public understanding or awareness of the benefits of DC systems can result in resistance or indifference towards adoption, necessitating targeted education and communication strategies to gain public support.
