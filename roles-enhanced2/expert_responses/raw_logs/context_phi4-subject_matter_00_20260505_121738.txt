1. **Interoperability Challenges**
   - The lack of standardized interfaces between DC systems and existing AC infrastructure can create significant integration challenges, leading to increased complexity and costs during implementation.

2. **Limited Awareness and Training**
   - There is insufficient awareness and technical training available for engineers and technicians on DC technologies, which hinders effective adoption and efficient operation in various industries.

3. **Economic Uncertainty**
   - The initial high capital expenditure required for transitioning to DC systems can deter investment, especially when long-term economic benefits are uncertain or difficult to quantify compared to established AC systems.

4. **Regulatory Hurdles**
   - Existing electrical codes and regulations are predominantly designed around AC systems, posing regulatory challenges that need addressing before widespread DC adoption can occur.

5. **Supply Chain Constraints**
   - The current supply chain for DC components is not as mature or extensive as for AC systems, leading to potential shortages and delays in sourcing necessary hardware like converters and cables.
