1. **Interoperability Challenges**
   - The integration of DC systems with existing AC infrastructure can face significant interoperability issues, particularly when it comes to ensuring seamless communication and compatibility between new DC components and legacy AC equipment.

2. **Cost Implications for Transition**
   - The initial costs associated with transitioning from traditional AC systems to DC, including the need for new hardware and redesign of electrical layouts, can be a substantial financial barrier for many organizations considering adoption.

3. **Skilled Workforce Shortage**
   - There is a limited pool of professionals who are trained or experienced in designing, installing, and maintaining DC systems. This shortage could slow down implementation efforts and increase project costs due to the need for specialized training programs.

4. **Regulatory Hurdles**
   - Existing electrical codes and standards predominantly cater to AC systems. The lack of comprehensive regulatory frameworks specifically addressing DC technology poses a barrier, as it can lead to delays in approvals and increased compliance costs.

5. **Public Perception and Acceptance**
   - Despite the potential benefits of DC systems, there may be resistance from stakeholders due to unfamiliarity or misconceptions about DC technologies. This could hinder adoption rates until public perception shifts towards recognizing the advantages of DC systems.
