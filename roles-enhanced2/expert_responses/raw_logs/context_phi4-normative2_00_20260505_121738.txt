1. **Grid Integration Complexity**  
   Transitioning from AC to DC systems requires significant changes to existing grid infrastructure, creating technical complexities and potential compatibility issues.

2. **Standardization Gaps**  
   The lack of unified standards for DC technology across regions can hinder widespread adoption by complicating interoperability and regulatory compliance.

3. **Economic Uncertainty**  
   Initial costs associated with transitioning to DC systems may deter investment due to uncertain return on investment timelines and economic feasibility in different market conditions.

4. **Skilled Workforce Deficit**  
   A shortage of professionals trained specifically in DC technologies can slow down implementation, leading to project delays and increased reliance on external expertise.

5. **Public Perception Challenges**  
   Limited public awareness and understanding of the benefits of DC systems may result in resistance to adoption, particularly in consumer-facing applications like residential energy use.
