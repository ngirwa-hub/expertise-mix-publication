5. **Decommissioning & End-of-Life Management:** Lack of established protocols and regulations for decommissioning DC infrastructure and managing end-of-life components presents an emerging environmental and regulatory risk.

1. **Lack of Standardized Component Safety Testing**
Current safety testing standards are largely geared towards AC systems. Adapting or creating new standards for DC components will be crucial but time-consuming.

2. **Cybersecurity Vulnerabilities in DC Microgrids**
DC microgrids, particularly those integrating renewable energy and edge computing, present novel cybersecurity attack vectors that require specific mitigation strategies.

3. **Limited Availability of Skilled Technicians**
A shortage of technicians trained in DC system installation, maintenance, and troubleshooting will hinder widespread adoption and increase operational costs.

4. **Retrofit Challenges in Existing Infrastructure**
Replacing existing AC infrastructure with DC presents significant logistical and financial barriers, especially in older buildings and industrial facilities.

5. **Interoperability Concerns Across Diverse DC Voltage Levels**
The emergence of multiple competing DC voltage standards could lead to compatibility issues and limit the scalability of DC systems.
