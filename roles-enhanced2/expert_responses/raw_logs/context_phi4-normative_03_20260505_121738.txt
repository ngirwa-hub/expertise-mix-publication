### New Barriers to DC Adoption

1. **Interoperability Challenges**
   - The integration of direct current (DC) systems with existing alternating current (AC) infrastructure can be complex due to differences in standards, protocols, and technologies. This lack of interoperability may hinder seamless transitions and increase costs for retrofitting or upgrading facilities.

2. **Supply Chain Limitations**
   - Current supply chains are predominantly tailored to AC components and systems. The transition to DC requires the development of new manufacturing processes and supply networks for DC-specific parts like converters, cables, and controllers, which could lead to initial shortages and increased costs.

3. **Skill Gap in Workforce**
   - There is a significant knowledge gap among electricians and engineers regarding DC technologies. This lack of expertise can impede installation, maintenance, and troubleshooting processes, leading to delays and potential safety issues during the transition period.

4. **Regulatory Uncertainty**
   - The regulatory environment for DC systems is still evolving, with many regions lacking clear guidelines or standards specific to DC applications. This uncertainty can deter investment and adoption due to concerns about compliance and future policy changes.

5. **Consumer Perception and Acceptance**
   - Public understanding of the benefits and safety of DC systems is limited. Without widespread awareness and acceptance, consumers may resist transitioning to DC solutions, particularly in residential settings where trust in new technologies is crucial for market penetration.
