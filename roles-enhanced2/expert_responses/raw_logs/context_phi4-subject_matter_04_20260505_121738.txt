1. **Grid Integration Complexity**
   - Integrating DC systems with existing AC grids poses technical challenges due to differences in voltage levels, frequency standards, and system stability requirements. This complexity can lead to increased costs and technical hurdles in achieving seamless interoperability.

2. **Standardization Gaps**
   - The lack of unified international standards for low and medium voltage DC technologies hinders widespread adoption. Discrepancies in regulations across regions create barriers for manufacturers and prevent uniform implementation, complicating global market penetration.

3. **Component Availability and Cost**
   - A limited availability of cost-effective and high-quality DC components such as converters and inverters can slow adoption rates. The higher initial costs associated with these components compared to their AC counterparts may deter investment in DC technologies.

4. **Technical Expertise Shortage**
   - There is a shortage of skilled professionals with expertise in designing, installing, and maintaining DC systems. This gap in technical knowledge within the workforce creates challenges for organizations looking to transition from AC to DC solutions.

5. **Public Perception and Awareness**
   - Limited public understanding and acceptance of DC technologies can act as a barrier. Misconceptions about safety, efficiency, or reliability may lead to resistance among consumers and stakeholders, affecting the adoption rate of DC systems in residential and commercial sectors.
