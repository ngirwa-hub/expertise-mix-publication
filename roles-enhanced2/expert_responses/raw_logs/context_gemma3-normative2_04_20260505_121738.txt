## New Barriers to DC Adoption (Shift2DC Expert Elicitation)

Here are five new barriers to DC adoption, considering my role as Senior Permitting and Regulatory Advisor:

1. **Harmonized International Standards Gap:** Lack of globally recognized and harmonized standards for DC systems creates uncertainty for manufacturers and project developers, hindering cross-border deployment and increasing regulatory friction.
2. **Legacy Grid Interoperability Concerns:** Integrating DC microgrids with existing AC grid infrastructure presents significant technical and permitting challenges, requiring complex protection schemes and potentially lengthy approval processes.
3. **Public Safety Perception & Regulation:** Public perception of DC systems, particularly regarding electrical safety, could lead to stricter permitting requirements and delays, requiring extensive public outreach and safety demonstration.
4. **Supply Chain Vulnerabilities:** Dependence on specialized components and materials for DC systems could create supply chain bottlenecks and increase project costs, especially if geopolitical factors influence availability.
5. **Insurance & Liability Frameworks:** Current insurance and liability frameworks are largely built around AC systems. Adapting these to DC presents a regulatory hurdle and may initially increase project costs.

1. **Harmonized Safety Standards**
Lack of globally recognized and harmonized safety standards specific to low and medium voltage DC systems creates uncertainty and hinders widespread adoption due to differing regulations and potential liability concerns.

2. **Retrofit Costs & Disruptions**
Replacing existing AC infrastructure with DC in established buildings and industrial facilities is expensive and disruptive, presenting a significant hurdle for adoption beyond new constructions.

3. **Lack of Trained Workforce**
A shortage of skilled technicians and engineers familiar with DC system design, installation, and maintenance will slow deployment and increase operational risks.

4. **Component Availability & Cost**
Limited availability and high cost of specialized DC components (cables, converters, power supplies) compared to readily available AC alternatives restricts scalability and affordability.

5. **Interoperability Challenges**
Ensuring interoperability between different DC systems and components from various manufacturers is crucial, but currently lacks standardized protocols and testing procedures.
