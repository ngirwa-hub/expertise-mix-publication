1. **Interoperability Challenges**  
   The integration of DC systems with existing AC infrastructure can be complex, requiring advanced interfaces or converters. This often leads to compatibility issues and increased costs, hindering seamless adoption.

2. **Standardization Gaps**  
   Lack of universally accepted standards for DC components and systems results in fragmented development efforts and creates uncertainty among stakeholders about which technologies to adopt.

3. **Skill Shortage**  
   There is a limited pool of professionals skilled in designing, installing, and maintaining DC systems. This shortage can slow down adoption as projects may face delays or require additional training resources.

4. **Economic Uncertainty**  
   The initial investment for DC infrastructure can be high due to the need for new components like converters and specialized wiring. Without clear economic incentives or proven cost-benefit analyses, potential adopters are hesitant to commit.

5. **Public Perception and Awareness**  
   Limited public understanding of the benefits and safety of DC systems can lead to resistance from end-users and stakeholders. Addressing misconceptions is crucial for gaining broader acceptance and support.
