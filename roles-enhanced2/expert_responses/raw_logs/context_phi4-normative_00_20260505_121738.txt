### Barriers to DC Adoption

1. **Integration with Existing AC Infrastructure**
   - Integrating DC systems into existing predominantly AC-based infrastructure poses significant technical challenges, requiring substantial retrofitting and coordination across various sectors.

2. **Lack of Standardization**
   - The absence of universally accepted standards for DC technologies hampers interoperability between different manufacturers' equipment, complicating implementation and scalability.

3. **High Initial Investment Costs**
   - Transitioning to DC systems involves considerable upfront costs in terms of new hardware, training, and system redesigns, which can deter adoption despite potential long-term savings.

4. **Knowledge and Skill Gaps**
   - A shortage of skilled professionals with expertise in DC technologies limits the capacity for effective implementation and maintenance, necessitating extensive education and training programs.

5. **Regulatory Uncertainty**
   - The evolving nature of regulations surrounding DC systems creates uncertainty for stakeholders, complicating compliance efforts and potentially delaying adoption due to unclear legal frameworks.
