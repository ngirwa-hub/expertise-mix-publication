1. **Interoperability Challenges**
   - The lack of standardized protocols for integrating various DC components can hinder seamless communication between devices from different manufacturers, complicating system design and operation.

2. **Skill Gap in Workforce**
   - A significant barrier is the shortage of skilled professionals trained specifically in DC technologies, which can delay implementation and increase project costs due to reliance on external expertise.

3. **Economic Viability Concerns**
   - The initial investment required for transitioning to DC systems can be prohibitive for some organizations, especially if short-term financial returns are uncertain compared to existing AC infrastructure.

4. **Regulatory Uncertainty**
   - Inconsistent or unclear regulations across regions regarding the use and implementation of DC technologies can create barriers to adoption by causing hesitation among stakeholders about compliance and future-proofing investments.

5. **Public Perception and Awareness**
   - Limited public understanding and acceptance of DC technology benefits may lead to resistance in adopting these systems, especially in residential areas where consumer buy-in is crucial for widespread implementation.
