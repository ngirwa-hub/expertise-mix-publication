1. **Grid Integration Complexity**
   - Integrating DC systems into existing AC power grids presents technical challenges, including compatibility issues with legacy infrastructure. This necessitates extensive modifications or hybrid solutions that could increase costs and complexity.

2. **Standardization Gaps**
   - The lack of standardized protocols for DC technologies hinders interoperability between different systems and manufacturers. Establishing industry-wide standards is crucial to facilitate widespread adoption but remains a significant barrier.

3. **Economic Viability Concerns**
   - While DC systems can offer efficiency benefits, the initial investment costs are often higher than AC alternatives. This economic barrier can deter stakeholders, particularly in cost-sensitive sectors like residential and small-scale commercial applications.

4. **Regulatory Uncertainty**
   - Ambiguities in regulatory frameworks for DC technologies create uncertainty for developers and investors. Clearer regulations and incentives are needed to encourage innovation and deployment of DC solutions across various industries.

5. **Public Perception and Awareness**
   - Limited public understanding and awareness of the benefits of DC systems can impede adoption. Educational initiatives are necessary to shift perceptions and demonstrate the potential advantages of transitioning to DC technologies in everyday applications.
