1. **Interoperability Challenges**
   - Integrating DC systems with existing AC infrastructure can be complex, requiring specialized equipment and protocols to ensure seamless operation across different voltage levels.

2. **Economic Viability Concerns**
   - The initial costs for deploying DC systems, including new hardware and training, may deter adoption unless long-term savings or incentives are clearly demonstrated.

3. **Standardization Deficits**
   - Lack of universally accepted standards for low and medium voltage DC can lead to compatibility issues, hindering widespread implementation and innovation.

4. **Market Maturity and Awareness**
   - Limited awareness and understanding of the benefits and applications of DC systems among stakeholders, including consumers and industry players, may slow adoption rates.

5. **Regulatory and Policy Barriers**
   - Inconsistent or outdated regulations can impede DC system deployment by creating legal uncertainties or additional compliance burdens for developers and operators.
